    Hello, my name is Julean. This website tells all facts about me. I actually grew up in the Philippines, now I'm teenager (16 y/o) with different personalities and life style. As a Filipina student here in New Zealand I'm proud to share what my community or culture looks like with all of you. Growing up in the Philippines I learned that life is actually meant to be celebrated through food, art or color, and full of creativity, usually all at once. Cooking became my first hobby because of my mother, and or is it I grew up smelling good food? I actually don't know (haha) — cooking is the most commonly known talent of Filipinos that believing part of Filipino culture. For me, cooking isn't just a hobby, it became my way to show love and care for my family and friends — serving them delicous food with pure love and happines. This hobby also brings us together, that helps us to connect, sharing thoughts and story, eat together, then talk about how our life is going lately.



    When I'm not in the kitchen, I'm now holding a pen or paint brush instead of pot and ladles. I really love sketching and painting, specifically animes, body, clothes, and different sunsets or ocean. It's my way of relaxing and forget all of my problems. For me, art is like a universal language. As much as I love cooking, I also love being creative. It serves as an tool for me to connect me with my self and my culture. Many Filipino's are naturally creative, through art they can express their feelings and to show the beauty of Philippines. This includes the handmade product of Filipino's (e.g basketry, bags, woodcarving, loom weaving, parols, and pottery). Also, through creativity they can show the unforgettable history of our country. Through painting, sculpture, music, and digital media, Filipinos use art to connect with their roots and shape their future. So by arts and crafts, we should learn how to showcase and give importance to our own talents.



    When I'm tired being inside the house, I used to play badminton and volleyball outside with my family and friends. Playing sports like this became the tool to conncet with each other. This hobby does not require any expensive equipment or training to be better, just proper communication and having enough courage to learn how to play. In volleyball, teamwork is very important to make success. While in badminton, requires determination to be better in playing. Playing these kind of sports helped me to go out to my comfort zone and live like others with communication and confidence. These are "lifelong activities" that can be played at home, in backyards, or at local community centres for decades. In the Philippines, most teenagers play badminton and volleyball using (pambahay), this is the casual clothing specifically intended to be worn only inside the home in the Philippines. To understand better, badminton and volleyball in the Philippines do not require expensive clothes, just wear everything you find comfortable.



    Wrapping this up, I guess my life in the Philippines and now living in New Zealand has taught me that no matter where I am, I still carry with me my culture, the talents I have, and the things that has always been part of me. Cooking adobo and sinigang with my family, painting sunsets, sketching humans, smashing shuttle cock and volleyball with friends that are also like my family — is the reason why I keep pushing through, standing strong and always finding happines. I wish that in every lifetime or universe rather, I hope I can still find the girl in me who enjoys most of her teenage life. I want to keep living like this, sharing my passions and maybe even inspire others to do what makes them happy and just enjoy their existence in this world.
